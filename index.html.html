What Half a Trillion Shillings Has Cost Kenyans

THE DEVOLUTION TAX
KES 491B
Total NGCDF allocation from 2013/14 to 2025/26, the devolution era. County governments have argued they receive less than their constitutional entitlement.
What this costs Kenyans: Every shilling to NGCDF before vertical division is a shilling less for 47 county governments. The High Court found that in 2016, NGCDF took KES 36B when the correct share should have been KES 25B. That KES 11B gap came directly from counties. Multiply across 13 years and the cumulative loss is staggering.
THE PATRONAGE PREMIUM
KES 192M+ Per MP
Average per constituency in 2025/26. Some get over KES 221M. More than entire ward budgets in many counties.
What this costs Kenyans: An MP controls more development money than most ward reps. Bursary allocations, contractor selection, project priorities all flow through MP structures. This converts the MP's office into a patronage machine. Constituents seeking school fees or contracts must go through the MP, not the county. The Auditor General has documented how some MPs use contractors for kickbacks.
üö® What the Auditor General Has Found
KES 1.3B
Unsupported expenditure across 99 constituencies in 2023/24. No receipts, no invoices. Samburu East: KES 156M. Emurua Dikirr: KES 102.5M.
KES 496M
Stalled and abandoned projects across 29 constituencies. Money spent with nothing built.
KES 26.7B
Unutilized funds sitting idle across all 290 constituencies as of June 2024. A recurring problem year after year.
Ghost students
Bursaries to non-existent students. Sabatia: KES 58M claimed, KES 38M accounted for. Narok West: 675 beneficiaries with no admission numbers. Some shared numbers. Some had no names.
10 of 12
NGCDF-funded schools in Kimilili found structurally unsound. A classroom in Aldai unfit for habitation. Children in buildings that could collapse.
KES 63M
Flagged in Nakuru Town East including KES 21M in questionable security spending. In Rongo, KES 2.5M for a laboratory that was never constructed.
These are not isolated findings. The Auditor General has flagged similar problems year after year, across different constituencies, regions, and parties. The structural incentive for misuse is embedded in the design: 290 spending streams, each shaped by one MP, with accountability mechanisms the Auditor General has called inadequate.
THE BURSARY TRAP
KES 15B/Year
Approximately KES 15 billion annually for bursaries. The most popular and politically powerful element of NGCDF.
What this costs Kenyans: Bursaries help real students. But the MP's office decides who gets them. In a country where education determines life trajectories, this creates dependency between constituents and their MP. It converts a constitutional right (Article 43) into a political gift. Speaker Wetang'ula has proposed consolidating all bursaries into a single body because the system is fragmented and politicized.
THE DUPLICATION COST
Two Parallel Systems
NGCDF builds roads, classrooms, health facilities. Counties do the same through their development plans.
What this costs Kenyans: Two systems in the same space, different accountability structures, no coordination. From 2017/18 to 2021/22, NGCDF built 2,102 chief's offices, 193 admin blocks, 61 KMTCs, 155 technical institutes. The Controller of Budget documented wastage from competition between MPs and governors.
THE OPPORTUNITY COST
KES 522B Over 22 Years
More than the SGR. More than all 47 counties received in equitable share in 2024/25. Nearly 4x the entire national health budget.
What this costs Kenyans: If KES 522B had gone through county governments with constitutionally established planning, procurement, and oversight, accountability would be fundamentally different. Counties have assemblies, PFM Act requirements, independent auditing. NGCDF routes money through MP-influenced Project Management Committees with weaker accountability.
THE CONSTITUTIONAL COST
3 Laws Struck Down
CDF 2013, AASDF 2015, NGCDF 2015 (High Court). Each time, Parliament created a replacement.
What this costs Kenyans: If Parliament can rename its way around unconstitutionality, the rule of law is optional. Every time Parliament recreates a struck-down fund, it teaches every institution that judicial declarations can be circumvented. The 2010 Constitution was built on courts as final arbiters. If that fails here, it fails everywhere.

Katiba Institute: Counsel in the Fight for Constitutional Fidelity

Katiba Institute has been at the centre of the NGCDF challenge from the beginning, serving as counsel in Constitutional Petition 178 of 2016. Through its advocate Waikwa Wanyoike, Katiba Institute argued the case before the High Court, secured the landmark 2024 declaration of unconstitutionality, and is now pursuing the appeal at the Supreme Court.

This is not an isolated case. The NGCDF challenge sits within Katiba's broader constitutional litigation strategy that has tested the boundaries of legislative power, defended devolution, and held government to the text of the 2010 Constitution across dozens of cases.

What makes this case distinctive is that it challenges one of the most politically powerful institutions in Kenyan politics. Taking this case forward requires the willingness to confront the entire legislature, sustained over a decade of delays, and the capacity to argue at every level of the court system.

Katiba's Key Legal Arguments

üìú
Two Unconstitutional Laws Reinforcing Each Other
Waikwa Wanyoike argued that the NGCDF Act and the Division of Revenue Act were being used to implement each other, both unconstitutional. The DRA netted NGCDF allocations from national revenue before equitable sharing, contrary to Articles 201 and 203.
‚öñÔ∏è
Revenue Netting Harms Counties Directly
Katiba demonstrated Section 4 provides for 2.5% of the national government's share, but the DRA allocated 2.5% of total national revenue before vertical division. In 2016, the fund took KES 36B when the proper calculation would have yielded KES 25B. Counties lost KES 11B in that year alone.
üèõÔ∏è
Parliament Cannot Be Both Legislator and Executive
Katiba challenged Sections 15, 19, 23, 43, and 53, arguing they give the National Assembly executive roles: approving Board members, Committee members, and overseeing project implementation. MPs controlling constituency development spending is an executive function exercised by legislators.
üèòÔ∏è
Devolution Means What It Says
The 2010 Constitution superseded the pre-2010 CDF logic by creating devolved governments. NGCDF reintroduces through ordinary statute a framework devolution was designed to replace, creating a parallel constituency system that competes with county functions under the Fourth Schedule.

Katiba's Litigation Journey

MAY 2016: PETITION FILED, CONSERVATORY ORDERS SECURED
Katiba Institute, as counsel, filed the petition and secured conservatory orders in July 2016. Certified as raising substantial questions of law and forwarded to the Chief Justice for multi-judge bench.
SEPT 2024: HIGH COURT VICTORY ON ALL GROUNDS
After eight years of delays, Katiba won a comprehensive declaration that the NGCDF Act was unconstitutional in its entirety. The bench accepted arguments on separation of powers, devolution, public finance, and Senate involvement.
FEB 2026: COURT OF APPEAL SETBACK
Court of Appeal reversed, finding analysis insufficient and remedy disproportionate. Only Section 43(9) severed. A setback, but the partial finding confirms the constitutional tension is real.
FEB 2026: SAME-DAY APPEAL TO SUPREME COURT
On the same day, Katiba filed a notice of appeal to the Supreme Court. Ten years. Three courts. KES 522 billion. The final word on NGCDF will come from Kenya's highest court.

Six Constitutional Problems with the NGCDF

‚öñÔ∏è
Separation of Powers Violated
Section 43 establishes NGCDF Committees for every constituency, members approved by the National Assembly. MPs identify, approve, and oversee development projects. Legislators performing executive functions.
üèòÔ∏è
Devolution Undermined
47 county governments manage local development. NGCDF creates a parallel system of 290 constituencies duplicating county functions: roads, classrooms, health facilities. Two systems, no coordination.
üí∞
Public Finance Breached
Article 203 requires equitable division between national and county governments. NGCDF funds are netted before vertical division. The DRA formula contradicts even the NGCDF Act's own text. Counties lose billions.
üèõÔ∏è
Senate Bypassed
The Senate represents county interests. The NGCDF Act concerns counties but was enacted without Senate involvement. High Court agreed. Court of Appeal disagreed. Supreme Court will decide.
üîÑ
Pattern of Legislative Defiance
CDF 2013 struck down. Parliament created NGCDF 2015. Challenged, then amended in 2022 and 2023 preserving the same structure. Parliament treats judicial declarations as problems to rename.
üó≥Ô∏è
The Patronage Machine
Over KES 200M per constituency annually. MPs influence bursaries, contractors, project priorities. Enormous incumbent advantage. This is why dismantling it is politically explosive and why it has survived 23 years across every administration.

High Court: 20 September 2024

NGCDF ACT DECLARED UNCONSTITUTIONAL IN ITS ENTIRETY
Justices Kimondo, Thande, and Aburili declared the NGCDF Act 2015 (as amended 2022, 2023) unconstitutional. Violated separation of powers, undermined devolution, created overlaps, enacted without Senate input.
OPERATIONS TO CEASE BY 30 JUNE 2026
~20 months for transition. All NGCDF projects, programmes, and activities to cease at midnight 30 June 2026. Court recognized ongoing projects, employees, and third-party contracts.
PETITION NOT MOOT DESPITE AMENDMENTS
Parliament's 2022 and 2023 amendments did not render the petition moot. Fundamental constitutional defects survived cosmetic changes. Court refused to let Parliament avoid review through continuous amendment.

Court of Appeal: 6 February 2026

HIGH COURT JUDGMENT SET ASIDE
Justices Musinga, Tuiyott, and Muchelule overturned the ruling. Lower court failed in constitutional analysis. Striking down the entire Act was disproportionate and inconsistent with severability.
NGCDF DOES NOT VIOLATE DEVOLUTION
Court of Appeal: NGCDF is national government expenditure approved through the Appropriations Act with multiple oversight layers. Does not offend the division of functions between national and county governments.
SENATE NOT REQUIRED
Senate involvement not required for the NGCDF Act. Directly contradicts the High Court. A core issue for the Supreme Court.
PARTIAL WIN: SECTION 43(9) SEVERED
Section 43(9) declared unconstitutional: tying fund manager terms to parliamentary election cycles. Even the Court of Appeal acknowledged the NGCDF engages separation of powers concerns.

A Decade-Long Constitutional Battle

2003
CDF Born: KES 1.3B to 210 Constituencies
CDF Act 2003, initiated by Hon. Muriuki Karue's private member's Bill, allocates 2.5% of national revenue to constituencies under MP control. Each gets KES 6M.
Feb 2015
First Victory: CDF 2013 Struck Down
Institute for Social Accountability v National Assembly: High Court declares CDF unconstitutional. Parliament responds by creating NGCDF under a new name.
2015-2016
Second Victory: AASDF Regulations Struck Down
Wanjiru Gikonyo challenges the Affirmative Action Social Development Fund Regulations, another MP-controlled funding scheme. Struck down.
4 May 2016
Katiba Files Petition 178: The NGCDF Challenge
Gikonyo and Opuot, with Katiba Institute as counsel, file the constitutional petition. Four grounds: separation of powers, devolution, public finance, Senate involvement. Conservatory orders granted July 2016.
2016-2024
Eight Years of Delays, While Billions Flow
Bench changes, deaths, transfers, stays. Over KES 300B flows through NGCDF during these eight years. Parliament amends in 2022 and 2023.
20 Sept 2024
High Court Victory: Entire NGCDF Act Unconstitutional
Justices Kimondo, Thande, Aburili rule for petitioners on every ground. Act declared unconstitutional. Operations to cease by 30 June 2026.
6 Feb 2026
Court of Appeal Reverses: NGCDF Largely Saved
Justices Musinga, Tuiyott, Muchelule set aside High Court judgment. Only Section 43(9) severed. NGCDF Act remains in force.
6 Feb 2026
Katiba Takes It to the Supreme Court
Same day: Katiba files notice of appeal to the Supreme Court. The final word on NGCDF's constitutionality comes from Kenya's highest court.

Four Questions for the Supreme Court

01
Does the NGCDF violate separation of powers?
The Divide
High Court: yes. Court of Appeal: mostly no, only Section 43(9). The Supreme Court decides whether 290 MPs can administer KES 55B annually through constituency committees.
02
Does the NGCDF undermine devolution?
The Divide
High Court: yes, parallel structure outside counties. Court of Appeal: no, national expenditure. The answer determines whether the 2010 Constitution's devolution promise is realized or permanently compromised.
03
Should the Senate have been involved?
The Divide
High Court: yes. Court of Appeal: no. This bicameral process question has implications far beyond NGCDF for every future Bill touching county interests.
04
Can courts strike down entire statutes or must they sever?
The Divide
Court of Appeal criticized striking down the entire Act. The Supreme Court will set the standard for proportionality in constitutional remedies across all future cases.
